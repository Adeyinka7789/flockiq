Executive Summary

Aladimeji Farms, a 25,000-bird broiler operation in Lagos, was struggling with recurring respiratory outbreaks that claimed 12-15% of each flock. Traditional monitoring methods meant outbreaks were only detected after birds showed clinical signs — often too late to prevent significant losses.

After implementing FlockIQ's AI-powered anomaly detection system, the farm now receives alerts 72 hours before symptoms appear, enabling early intervention. Within three cycles, mortality dropped from 12% to 7.2% — a 40% reduction — saving the farm over ₦12 million annually.

The Challenge

Before FlockIQ, Aladimeji Farms relied on manual daily checks and visual inspection. By the time a sick bird was spotted, the disease had often spread to entire houses. Key challenges included:

  • 15-20% mortality during respiratory outbreaks
  • No early warning system for disease detection
  • Missed vaccination schedules due to manual tracking
  • Inconsistent feed and water consumption records
"
Before FlockIQ, we were always reacting to problems after they happened. Birds would get sick, and by the time we noticed, we'd already lost hundreds. It was heartbreaking and expensive.
Mr. Adeboye Aladimeji
Chairman, Aladimeji Farms

The Solution

Aladimeji Farms deployed FlockIQ's full platform, including:

  • AI Anomaly Detection — Real-time Z-score analysis of mortality, water consumption, and feed intake
  • Vaccination Scheduler — Automated reminders for all breed-specific vaccines
  • SMS Alerts — Critical notifications sent directly to farm managers' phones
  • Daily Logging — Mobile-first data entry for mortality, feed, and water
